The Orignal Singers to "Jesus Is Just Alright"

Bio

Check out their discography! It all started in the late 1960's: Art Reynolds took the five best singers of choir, at the time; and started The Art Reynolds Singers! During the recording of their first album, “Tellin’ It Like It Is," Gene Parsons (drummer for The Byrds, a popular rock band) was present; and he witnessed what would be the iconic and nationally acclaimed song, "Jesus Is Just Alright," (1966). This song was a riposte to John Lennon's comment (and became more popular than his comment), “The Beatles are more popular than Jesus.” Of course, Gene introduced the song to The Byrds, who later recorded the same in 1969; which the twist they placed on their version, provided a template to cover of the most popular version recording of all time, performed by The Doobie Brothers, in 1972 (who also added verses and their own twist). Many other groups and bands would record their own version to the "Jesus Is Just Alright," most using The Doobie Brothers' version; but the origin was performed by The Art Reynolds Singers, whose band leader, and author to "Jesus Is Just Alright," could not have been happier to the outcome and effect of their mesage: The Message of Jesus Christ spread globally.
